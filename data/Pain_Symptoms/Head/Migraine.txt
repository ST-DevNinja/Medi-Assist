Migraine is a neurological condition characterized by intense, debilitating headaches often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light or sound. Migraines may occur with or without aura (sensory disturbances).

Causes:
- Genetic predisposition
- Hormonal fluctuations (e.g., menstruation)
- Stress and anxiety
- Certain foods or drinks (aged cheese, caffeine, alcohol)
- Sleep disturbances

Symptoms:
- Throbbing or pulsing pain, typically on one side of the head
- Visual disturbances (flashing lights, blind spots)
- Sensitivity to light, sound, or smells
- Nausea and vomiting
- Duration from 4 to 72 hours

Treatment:
- Triptans or ergotamines
- NSAIDs (e.g., naproxen)
- Preventive medications (beta-blockers, anticonvulsants)
- Lifestyle changes and avoiding triggers

When to See a Doctor:
- Frequent or severe migraines
- New neurological symptoms
- Sudden, severe headache
