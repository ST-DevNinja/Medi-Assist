Title: Chest Pain

Definition:
Chest pain is a discomfort or pain that you feel anywhere along the front of your body between your neck and upper abdomen. It may be a symptom of serious heart problems or other conditions.

Symptoms:
- Tightness or pressure in the chest
- Burning or aching pain
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness or nausea
- Pain radiating to the arm, neck, or back

Causes:
- Heart attack or angina
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
- Muscle strain
- Pulmonary embolism
- Anxiety or panic attacks

Diagnosis:
A physical exam, electrocardiogram (ECG), blood tests, chest X-ray, and sometimes stress tests or angiography are used to determine the cause.

Treatment:
- Emergency care for heart-related issues
- Medications like nitroglycerin, aspirin, or antacids
- Lifestyle changes
- Anxiety management techniques
- Surgery (if needed for cardiac issues)

Prevention:
- Managing blood pressure and cholesterol
- Quitting smoking
- Regular exercise
- Reducing stress
- Healthy diet
