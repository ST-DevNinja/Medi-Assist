Angina is chest pain or discomfort caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle. It is a symptom of coronary artery disease and may feel like pressure, squeezing, or heaviness.

Causes:
- Coronary artery disease (CAD)
- Atherosclerosis
- Physical exertion or emotional stress
- Smoking, hypertension, and high cholesterol

Symptoms:
- Pressure or tightness in chest
- Pain spreading to shoulders, arms, neck, or jaw
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue or dizziness
- Typically triggered by exertion and relieved by rest

Treatment:
- Nitroglycerin and beta-blockers
- Lifestyle modifications (diet, exercise, smoking cessation)
- Statins for cholesterol control
- Angioplasty or coronary artery bypass surgery in severe cases

When to See a Doctor:
- New or worsening chest pain
- Pain lasting more than a few minutes
- Associated with nausea, shortness of breath, or fainting
