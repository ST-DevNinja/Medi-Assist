Abdominal Cramps

Abdominal cramps are sudden, tight feelings of pain in the stomach or intestines. They can occur due to a wide range of causes, from minor digestive issues to serious medical conditions.

Causes:
- Indigestion or overeating
- Gastroenteritis (stomach flu)
- Menstrual cramps in women
- Food poisoning or intolerance (e.g., lactose intolerance)
- Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)
- Inflammatory Bowel Disease (Crohnâ€™s disease, ulcerative colitis)
- Appendicitis (if severe and localized to the lower right abdomen)
- Urinary tract infections

Symptoms:
- Sharp, cramping pain in the abdominal region
- Bloating and gas
- Nausea or vomiting
- Diarrhea or constipation
- Fever (in infections)
- Pain worsening after eating or during menstruation

Diagnosis:
- Physical examination and patient history
- Blood tests for infection or inflammation
- Stool tests for pathogens
- Abdominal ultrasound or CT scan
- Endoscopy or colonoscopy if chronic

Treatment:
- Rest and hydration
- Antispasmodics or pain relievers (e.g., acetaminophen, ibuprofen)
- Dietary modifications (avoid triggers like dairy, spicy food)
- Antibiotics if infection is present
- Hormonal therapy for menstrual cramps
- Surgery for conditions like appendicitis

When to See a Doctor:
- Persistent or worsening pain
- Blood in stool or vomit
- High fever or chills
- Inability to eat or drink
- Severe tenderness or swelling of the abdomen
