Kidney pain, also known as renal pain, is a type of discomfort or pain that occurs in the area of the kidneys, which are located in the upper back, just below the ribcage. 
The pain can be felt on one or both sides of the back, and it may radiate to the abdomen or groin area.

--Possible causes of kidney pain include:

Kidney stones (nephrolithiasis): small, hard mineral deposits that can cause severe pain, nausea, and vomiting
Kidney infection (pyelonephritis): a bacterial infection that can cause fever, chills, and pain in the back and abdomen
Kidney inflammation (nephritis): a condition that can cause pain, swelling, and damage to the kidneys
Polycystic kidney disease (PKD): a genetic disorder that can cause cysts to form on the kidneys, leading to pain and kidney damage
Urinary tract infection (UTI): a bacterial infection that can cause pain, burning, and frequent urination
Kidney cancer: a rare but serious condition that can cause pain, weight loss, and other symptoms


--Symptoms of kidney pain may include:

Dull, aching pain in the upper back or abdomen
Sharp, stabbing pain that radiates to the groin or abdomen
Pain that worsens with movement or activity
Nausea and vomiting
Fever and chills
Blood in the urine or cloudy urine
Frequent or painful urination


--Treatment options for kidney pain depend on the underlying cause, but may include:

Pain relief medications, such as acetaminophen or ibuprofen
Antibiotics to treat bacterial infections
Surgery to remove kidney stones or repair damaged kidneys
Dialysis or kidney transplantation in severe cases of kidney damage or failure
Lifestyle changes, such as staying hydrated, managing stress, and avoiding certain foods or activities that can exacerbate kidney pain


--When to seek medical attention for kidney pain:

Severe, sudden onset pain
Pain that worsens over time or is accompanied by other symptoms, such as fever, chills, or vomiting
Blood in the urine or cloudy urine
Difficulty starting or stopping urination
Pain that radiates to the abdomen or groin area
History of kidney disease or kidney stones
If you are experiencing kidney pain, it is essential to seek medical attention promptly to determine the underlying cause and receive proper treatment. 
A healthcare professional can perform a physical exam, take a medical history, and order diagnostic tests, such as imaging studies or blood tests, to diagnose and manage kidney pain.
